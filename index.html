Basic Information

  • Topic: Environmental Regulation and Firm's Green Innovation: Evidence From China
    • Conductor: Prof. Jingbo Cui, Economics and Management School, Wuhan University, China.
    • Assistant: Zhenxuan Wang, Economics and Management School, Wuhan University, China.
  • Our research focuses on the evidence-based relationship between firms' decisions and environmental regulation. We study the impact of a unique environmental regulatory policy called mandatory participation in Cleaner Production Audit (CPA) programs on green innovation of Chinese listed companies.

The Introduction of the Cleaner Production Audit

  • Earliest-launch-time: 2002
  • Responsible Organization: Ministry of Environmental Protection, China
  • Aim: To Address environmental problems that accompany rapid industrial growth, through regulating pollution-intensive firms in various sectors
  • Obligation: Every participated company is obligated to disclose its pollutant emissions, conduct firm-wide CPA projects, and pass an assessment and an acceptance inspection conducted by local environmental agencies.

Key Point and Basic Results

  • We distinguish environmental patents from others by matching the Chinese Patent Database with the green patent category reported by WIPO and created the firm-by-year green patent panel datasets. In the process of matching, we also classify the green patents into seven categories.
  • In order to investigate the characteristics of different industries, we collected and cleaned the industry-level data about energy consumption and pollutant emission. We employed a Difference-in-Difference approach to verify the positive effect of mandatory CPA participation on firms green innovation.
  • We identify the different kinds of cooperation body associated with its relative parent company to test the direct and indirect effects on green innovation.
  • Using the empirical results, we find a significant positive link between CPA participation and firmsâ€™ application for green patents. The stimulative effect is stronger on the firms of higher energy intensity and waste emission.
